Access Control Policy

Purpose:
This Access Control Policy establishes requirements for managing, approving, granting, reviewing, monitoring, and revoking access to the AegisSecure Cloud Platform (ASCP) in alignment with NIST SP 800-171 Rev 2 and Zero Trust principles.

Scope:
This policy applies to all Aegis users, administrators, contractors, service accounts, automation processes, and devices accessing or interacting with ASCP, the CUI Processing Enclave, or related project enclaves.

Roles & Responsibilities:
• Authorizing Official (AO) – Approves risk, major access exceptions.
• System Owner (SO) – Ensures access aligns with business needs.
• ISSM – Oversees access governance; approves privileged roles.
• ISSO – Grants/revokes access; performs quarterly access reviews.
• Identity Admin – Manages Entra ID GCC High identities and CA enforcement.
• Keycloak Admin – Manages enclave RBAC and service account credentials.
• DevSecOps – Ensures technical controls enforce least privilege.
• Users – Use only approved credentials/devices; safeguard accounts.

Policy Statements:
1. All authentication to ASCP shall occur through Entra ID GCC High, federated into Keycloak.
2. ASCP must enforce least privilege and Zero Trust principles for all user and service access.
3. Users must authenticate via MFA using a FIPS-approved factor.
4. Devices accessing ASCP must be Intune-managed, compliant, and located within the United States.
5. Privileged access must require:
   • Just-In-Time (JIT) elevation
   • Privileged Access Workstation (PAW)
   • Session logging and SIEM monitoring
6. Reviewers may access CUI documents; privileged roles shall not have access to CUI data.
7. Project enclaves may use ASCP APIs in metadata-only mode; no CUI export is permitted.
8. All access changes require proper documentation, approval, and tracking.
9. Quarterly access reviews must be conducted by the ISSO and approved by the ISSM.
10. Access violations shall trigger security review and potential revocation.

Control Alignment:
• NIST SP 800-171 Rev 2 – 3.1.1 through 3.1.22

Enforcement:
Violations of this policy may result in disciplinary action, access revocation, and incident reporting.
